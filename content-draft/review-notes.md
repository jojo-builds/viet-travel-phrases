# Vietnamese Travel Phrasebook Draft — Review Notes

- Total scenarios: 10
- Total phrases: 70

## Self-review summary
I reviewed all romanizations with one test in mind: would a monolingual English speaker say something close enough that a Vietnamese listener could usually recognize it in a travel context?

### Romanizations corrected in self-review
- Replaced accidental corrupted syllable text `নাই` with `NAI` in five entries so the guide stays readable to English speakers:
  - `taxi-4` — `dee doong NAI dee`
  - `price-1` — `gai NAI bao nyew`
  - `price-7` — `toy lay gai NAI`
  - `store-5` — `muh gai NAI zoop toy`
  - `hotel-4` — `fong NAI koh nong gwah`
- Kept the romanization style intentionally simple rather than linguistically exact. For example, forms like `khong`, `dook`, `goy`, and `nyew` are not technical transliteration; they are meant to be easy for an English-speaking tourist to approximate quickly.
- Preferred recognizable spoken rhythm over strict sound mapping. Example choices include `kahm UHN`, `sin CHOW`, and `bahk SIU`.

## Dialect / content notes
- Phrases were written toward Southern Vietnamese usage, especially the kind a traveler would hear around Ho Chi Minh City.
- A few phrases are standard nationwide rather than uniquely Southern, which is intentional for app usability.
- `bạc xỉu` is especially Southern and fits the coffee scenario well.
- `Dạ` is included because it is highly useful as a polite acknowledgment in the south, even though exact frequency and nuance vary by age and situation.
- Some pronunciation guides necessarily flatten sounds that English does not handle well, especially `ư`, final consonants, and tone differences. The current draft favors practical usability over precision.
