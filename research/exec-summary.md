# Executive Summary

- The biggest market gap is **not another Vietnamese learning app**; it is a **tourist-first offline phrase tool** that gets users to the right phrase instantly in real situations.
- The most frequent pain points across Ling, Drops, Pimsleur, Mondly, Google Translate, and generic phrasebooks are: **slow retrieval, subscription friction, weak tourist scenario coverage, unclear pronunciation help, and unreliable/unclear offline use**.
- Our strongest defensible angle is the combination of **offline-first + real Vietnam travel scenarios + romanized pronunciation that actually helps English speakers say the phrase**.
- The best ASO territory to own is the intersection of **“Vietnamese phrasebook,” “Vietnam travel phrases,” and “offline Vietnamese”** rather than generic “learn Vietnamese” keywords.
- Core product rule: do **not** compete on lesson depth or feature count; win by being the fastest, clearest, least stressful way for a first-time tourist to say the right thing in Vietnam.
