## Offer Framing Summary

### Recommended master framing

SpeakLocal should be sold as a practical travel phrasebook, not a language-learning app.

Recommended master promise:

The phrasebook for live travel moments when typing into a translator is too slow.

Recommended supporting frame:

Tourist-first, offline-first, scenario-first.

Recommended funnel sentence:

Start free with the phrases travelers reach for first. Unlock the full phrasebook once you want broader coverage for the whole trip.

Recommended product reason to choose SpeakLocal:

It is made for the car, counter, check-in, menu, and confusion moments where a traveler needs a fast, usable phrase right now, not a study tool and not a blank translation box.

### Viet v2 customer-facing frame

Viet Phrases is a travel phrasebook for Vietnam. It helps travelers handle the moments where they actually need language support: rides, check-in, ordering, prices, directions, misunderstandings, and the small recovery moments that slow a trip down.

Recommended one-line frame:

Fast Vietnam phrase help for rides, food, check-in, directions, and the live moments where the trip gets awkward.

### Tagalog customer-facing frame

Tagalog Phrases follows the same frame for the Philippines. It should feel like the same product family, not a different promise.

Recommended one-line frame:

Fast Philippines phrase help for rides, food, check-in, directions, courtesy, and the live moments where the trip gets awkward.

Recommended positioning rule:

- In App Store identity, lead with `Tagalog` because language intent matters.
- In subtitle, body copy, screenshots, and website destination pages, keep `Philippines` visible because the traveler is buying for the trip, not for abstract language study.

### Free versus premium distinction

Free should feel genuinely usable, not crippled.

Recommended offer shape to keep consistent everywhere:

- Free unlocks the starter categories and starter depth inside the most common travel situations.
- Premium adds both more depth inside those starter situations and entirely new higher-stress categories for the rest of the trip.
- Premium is not just `more of the same`. It is the jump from core basics to fuller trip coverage.

Recommended free-tier framing:

- Start with roughly 150 useful phrases across the travel situations people hit first.
- Use audio, search, saved phrases, and offline access from the start.
- Handle greetings, food, prices, rides, hotel basics, directions, and other core travel moments without paying first.

Recommended premium framing:

- Unlock roughly 750 phrases in the fuller traveler library.
- Get deeper coverage inside starter categories and unlock additional high-stress categories such as arrival, payments, help, SIM issues, pharmacy basics, and emergencies.
- Extend the app from a solid starter pack into a fuller travel companion for the whole trip.

### Why the app is useful before upgrade

- The free tier already solves real trip moments: greeting people, ordering, asking the price, taking a ride, finding your way, and checking in.
- Audio, pronunciation, search, saved phrases, and offline access make the free tier practical in the live moment.
- Free use builds trust because the app works before asking for money.

### Why premium is worth paying for

- Premium changes the app from "good for the basics" to "ready for the whole trip."
- The value is breadth and confidence: more categories, more recovery phrases, and better coverage when travel gets messy.
- Premium should be framed as fuller trip coverage under stress, not just more phrases for more money.
- One small one-time unlock feels fair because the app is utility software, not an ongoing subscription relationship.

### Recommended messaging guardrails

- Say `travel phrasebook`, `travel phrases`, `useful phrases`, `full library`, `for the trip`, and `for real travel moments`.
- Avoid `learn Vietnamese`, `learn Tagalog`, `master the language`, `course`, `lessons`, or `fluency`.
- Keep the promise about practical usage speed, not transformation or identity.

## Pricing Recommendation Brief

### Why one-time unlock is better than subscription for v2 proof

- This is a utility travel product, not an ongoing habit product.
- Travelers understand one small unlock for a phrasebook much faster than a recurring subscription.
- One-time pricing removes extra explanation, billing anxiety, and refund friction from the first monetization test.
- The current goal is proof of paid demand, not maximizing monetization complexity.
- A one-time unlock also matches the product architecture direction: offline-friendly, backend-light, and simple to support.

### Current price lock

- Current repo source of truth is `$4.99` one-time.
- This document should not be used to reopen price testing on its own.
- Use `docs/VIET_PREMIUM_EXPANSION_PLAN.md` to judge whether the premium-content depth supports the locked price honestly.
- If the live build still sits at `117 starter / 29 premium / 146 total`, keep customer copy generic about the full pack or use current live counts. Do not publish `150 / 750` copy early.

### Future price-change rule

- Any move away from `$4.99` must be a deliberate product decision reflected first in the root docs and the Vietnam premium expansion plan.
- Any move to `200 / 1000` must be treated the same way: deliberate, explicit, and not implied from this messaging document alone.
