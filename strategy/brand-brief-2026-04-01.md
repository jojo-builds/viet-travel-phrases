# Brand Brief — Travel Language App Umbrella Brand
**Date:** 2026-04-01

## Decision Summary
The Vietnamese Travel Phrasebook app should be positioned as the **first product inside a broader travel-language brand**, not as a standalone one-off app.

## Recommended Brand Name
# Soft Landing
**Tagline:** Language tools for smoother arrivals.

## Why this name
- emotionally resonant for travelers
- calm, premium-light, and practical
- works across multiple countries and products
- strong fit for app + newsletter + content brand + future tool ecosystem

## Backup name
**Carry On Tongue** — stronger personality, slightly more playful, still scalable.

---

## Core Audience
### Primary audience
- serial travelers
- digital nomads in Asia
- independent trip planners
- solo travelers and couples who organize their own trips
- travelers who want competence, not fluency

### Not the primary audience
- package tourists outsourcing everything
- classroom-style language learners seeking full fluency first

## Why this audience
This group experiences the travel friction the product actually solves: food ordering, transport, clarification, politeness, check-in, and on-the-ground problem solving. They are also more likely to:
- follow the brand long-term
- use multiple country apps over time
- recommend the app to others
- care about practical build-in-public content

---

## Brand Promise
**Move with confidence.**

This is the clearest 3-word promise because it captures the emotional and functional outcome: less hesitation, less confusion, better movement through real places.

---

## Positioning Statement
Soft Landing helps independent travelers move through new places with more confidence by giving them practical, offline-first phrase tools and real local-language guidance built from field use, not classroom theory.

---

## Brand Personality
### Voice
- direct
- grounded
- observant
- useful
- quietly confident
- anti-hype

### Should feel like
A smart traveler-builder living in the city, testing what works, and handing over the useful parts.

### Should not feel like
- cartoon language app energy
- generic startup founder posting
- glossy tourism brochure copy
- academic language-learning brand

---

## Visual Direction
### Look and feel
Between modern travel media and utility software:
- airy
- calm
- field-tested
- high-trust
- minimal but not sterile

### Palette direction
- deep navy / charcoal-blue
- off-white / stone / sand neutrals
- muted jade or sea-glass accent
- optional coral accent for warmth

### Design cues
- strong whitespace
- concise headlines
- phrase-card layouts
- maps / wayfinding / field-note details
- lived-in local photography over stock tourism imagery

### Competitive contrast
- **vs Duolingo:** less playful, less loud, more adult, more useful in context
- **vs Lonely Planet:** less guidebook-dense, more product-native, cleaner and lighter

---

## Content Engine Summary
### 5 permanent pillars
1. **The Friction Point** — where travel breaks due to language
2. **How Locals Actually Say It** — practical language vs textbook language
3. **Field Test Dispatches** — real-world testing from Da Nang
4. **Build the Tool in Public** — thoughtful product-building process
5. **Better Trip Systems** — prep, checklists, and practical travel operations

### Platform priority
- **Primary now:** X + Substack
- **Later expansion:** TikTok / YouTube Shorts for spoken phrase contrast and field clips

---

## Da Nang Advantage
Da Nang is not just background. It is proof.

It gives the brand:
- field-tested authority
- specific local observations others cannot easily replicate
- a steady stream of real use cases
- credibility that the product is shaped by lived reality

Use it early and consistently, but as supporting proof rather than the full identity.

---

## Strategic Rule
The account must never drift into sounding like a product page.

The brand wins if people think:
**“This is one of the few travel accounts actually helping people move through places better.”**

If that happens, the Vietnamese app becomes the first trusted tool in a long-term multi-language product line.
