# 90-Day Content Calendar Template
**Brand:** Multi-language travel phrasebook brand (Vietnam first)  
**Window:** 90 days pre-launch / early brand-building  
**Primary channels:** X, Substack  
**Secondary later:** TikTok, YouTube Shorts  

---

## How to use this template
This calendar is built for the pre-launch period where the goal is to:
1. define the account clearly
2. build trust through useful field-tested content
3. attract the right audience before the app ships
4. convert early attention into waitlist/download/review momentum

### Weekly channel baseline
- **X originals:** 4-5 per week
- **X replies:** 25-40 per week
- **X threads:** 1-2 per week
- **Substack:** 1 issue per week
- **Field-test posts:** 2 per week minimum

### Weekly content mix
- 2 Friction Point posts
- 1 Local/Field Test post
- 1 Build in Public post
- 1 Trip Systems / Prep post
- 1 Thread or Substack excerpt

## Weekly X Reply Plan (applies across all 12 weeks)
### Target accounts
- Asia travel creators
- Vietnam / Da Nang local voices
- digital nomads in SEA
- practical language creators
- indie app builders discussing product/process

### Reply formula
Each reply should do one of these:
- add a field observation
- add a specific travel example
- sharpen a vague point with practical detail
- share one thing learned from Da Nang/product testing

Avoid generic networking replies.

## Weekly Scorecard Template
Use this every week.

### Metrics to track
- X follower growth
- average impressions per original post
- bookmarks/saves on utility posts
- reply-to-profile-click conversion (qualitative estimate if needed)
- Substack subscriber growth
- click-through rate to waitlist/app page
- number of high-signal replies/DMs from real travelers

### Questions to review
1. Which post made the account identity clearer?
2. Which post got attention but not trust?
3. Which field-test observation should become a recurring format?
4. Did product mentions feel natural or promotional?
5. What travel friction point should be emphasized next week?

### X
- 35% friction/problem posts
- 25% field-test/local insight
- 20% trip systems
- 15% build in public
- 5% direct CTA/product push

### Substack
- 40% trip systems + practical frameworks
- 25% field dispatches / local notes
- 20% product logic / build breakdowns
- 15% direct pre-launch or launch conversion

## Success Benchmarks for the First 90 Days
Reasonable early indicators:
- 300-800 highly relevant X followers if execution is strong
- 50-200 Substack subscribers
- first core audience cluster: Vietnam travelers, SEA nomads, practical travel builders
- repeat engagement on recurring field-test posts
- enough signal to identify which pillar converts best to app intent

The real win is not vanity growth. It is creating an audience that understands the category: **travel language for real trips**.
